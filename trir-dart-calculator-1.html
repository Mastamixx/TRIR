What these numbers mean

TRIR (Total Recordable Incident Rate) measures how many OSHA-recordable injuries and illnesses your site had, normalized to a standard of 100 full-time workers over a year.

TRIR = (Recordable incidents × 200,000) ÷ Total hours worked

DART Rate narrows that down to the more serious cases — ones involving Days Away, Restricted duty, or job Transfer — and is often watched more closely by insurers and corporate safety programs.

DART Rate = (DART cases × 200,000) ÷ Total hours worked

The constant 200,000 represents 100 employees working 40 hours a week, 50 weeks a year — the baseline OSHA uses so rates are comparable across companies of any size.

Don't have exact "total hours worked" on hand? Use the "Calculate from headcount" toggle above — enter your employee count and an average annual hours figure (2,000 is a common full-time estimate), and the tool will estimate total hours for you.
